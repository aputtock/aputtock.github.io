Master's Degree in Information Studies

2024-Present
McGill University, Montréal Québec

The Data Science stream of McGill University’s Master of Information Studies (MIS) program offers a specialized path for students interested in harnessing the power of data to solve complex problems. This stream combines foundational knowledge of information management with advanced skills in data analysis, machine learning, and computational techniques.

Students in this stream gain expertise in data-driven decision-making, data visualization, statistical analysis, and the use of algorithms to process and interpret large datasets. The curriculum covers a wide range of topics, including data modeling, database management, data ethics, and programming languages like Python and R. Additionally, students explore how data science intersects with other fields such as business, healthcare, and social sciences.

Through practical coursework and hands-on projects, students learn to work with real-world data, preparing them to address challenges in areas such as predictive analytics, artificial intelligence, and big data. The program emphasizes the ethical implications of data science, ensuring that graduates are not only technically proficient but also responsible stewards of data.

Graduates of the Data Science stream are well-equipped for careers in various industries, including technology, finance, healthcare, and consulting. They can pursue roles like data scientist, data analyst, machine learning engineer, and business intelligence analyst, or continue their studies in related fields.


Diploma of Engineering Technology in Automated Systems Electromechanics

2004-2009
Rosemont Vocational Center, Montréal Québec

A Diploma of Engineering Technology (DEP) in Automated Systems Electromechanics is a specialized program designed to equip students with the skills and knowledge necessary to work in the rapidly evolving fields of automation, robotics, and electromechanical systems. This program typically blends theoretical learning with practical training, focusing on the integration of electrical, mechanical, and computer systems to design, troubleshoot, and maintain automated systems.

Students gain expertise in areas such as electrical circuits, control systems, programmable logic controllers (PLCs), robotics, sensors, and actuators. They also study industrial automation, mechatronics, and process control, gaining hands-on experience in labs and workshops using state-of-the-art equipment. The curriculum typically includes courses in electrical engineering principles, mechanical design, automation technology, and systems integration.

In addition to technical skills, students develop problem-solving abilities, critical thinking, and an understanding of safety standards and industry regulations. They learn how to diagnose and repair electromechanical systems, as well as how to optimize and automate industrial processes to improve efficiency and productivity.

Graduates of a DEP in Automated Systems Electromechanics are prepared to enter a variety of industries, including manufacturing, robotics, automotive, energy, and process automation. Career opportunities include roles such as automation technician, control systems technician, maintenance engineer, and electromechanical technician. The program provides a solid foundation for students wishing to pursue further studies or professional certifications in related fields.


Honour Bachelor's Degree in Labour Studies

2009-2014
McMaster University, Hamilton Ontario

The Labour Studies Honours degree at McMaster University is a comprehensive program that explores the dynamics of work, employment, and labor movements in a global context. This interdisciplinary program delves into issues such as workers' rights, the impact of globalization on labor, workplace inequality, collective bargaining, and social justice. Students are introduced to the historical, economic, and political frameworks that shape labor markets, as well as the policies and institutions that influence working conditions.

Through a combination of theoretical courses and practical research, the program encourages students to critically analyze the role of labor in society and its intersection with issues like gender, race, and class. Students gain an understanding of both the challenges workers face and the strategies employed by labor organizations to address these issues.

The Honours degree also emphasizes research and offers opportunities for hands-on experience through internships or fieldwork. Graduates are well-prepared for careers in labor advocacy, policy analysis, human resources, social services, and other sectors that require a deep understanding of labor relations and social change. The program provides a solid foundation for those wishing to pursue further graduate studies or engage in professional careers that focus on labor and social justice issues.


Ontario Secondary School Diploma

2004-2009
Sir John A MacDonald, Hamilton Ontario

The Ontario Secondary School Diploma (OSSD) is the high school graduation diploma awarded to students in Ontario, Canada, who successfully complete the required coursework and exams.

To earn the OSSD, students must complete a set number of compulsory and elective courses, including English, mathematics, science, and social studies.

They are also required to pass the Ontario Secondary School Literacy Test (OSSLT) and complete 40 hours of community service. The OSSD is recognized as a high school diploma, enabling students to pursue further education, enter the workforce, or apply for apprenticeships.
